Grade 12 Science Curriculum Reform

A PISA-aligned competency framework and a Cosmology + Optics pilot block, designed at the meso level.

This site collects the working documents for the Grade 12 science curriculum reform: a shared competency framework (decided 17 June 2026), and the systematic meso-design of the first pilot block (Cosmology + Optics, starting 17 August 2026).

The framework anchors the school's existing Waldorf science curriculum to the PISA 2025 scientific-literacy competencies, adapted to Grade 12 developmental modes. The pilot block tests cross-system capstone-question integration as the first concrete instance of the framework in use.

Primary deliverables

Working group documents

  • 17 June 2026 Grade 12 Science Competency Framework The shared spine. Six PISA 2025 competencies preserved verbatim; sub-abilities rewritten for Grade-12 developmental modes. Worked examples on S1 and E3; pointers for S2/S3/E1/E2.
  • Plan · For four sessions Jun 24 – Jul 29 Meso-Design Plan — Cosmology + Optics Pilot Block How the working group will systematically decide the seven meso outputs (competency selection, deep question, phase structure, bridge questions, capstone, coordination protocol, operationalisations) across four sessions before the pilot starts.
  • Session 1 tool · 24 June 2026 Cosmology Block — Possibility-Space Map A concept map of what a Grade-12 cosmology block could hold, organized into six themes, each node tagged by which deep-question candidate (1 epistemological / 2 stardust) and which PISA competency it serves. Filter and explore to inform the Session-1 decisions on competency selection (A) and the deep question (B).
