# Session 1 — Deep-Question Side-by-Side Review

*Working-group input paper for Session 1 (Wed Jun 24). The two candidate deep questions, presented for silent comparison before the fork-resolution mechanic. Companion to the [Meso-Design Plan](meso-design-plan-cosmology-optics-pilot.md), §Appendix.*

---

## Status of this page — read before the table

This is an **input working paper, not a ratified document**. Five things it is *not*:

1. **The phrasings are illustrative, not final.** Each candidate carries one provisional question sentence so the fork operates on questions rather than abstract themes. The *final* wording — including any hybrid — is fixed only at the moment of decision in Session 1.
2. **These two are not exhaustive.** Session 1 may synthesise a **third** question. The table is the two live drafts the group enters with, not a closed ballot.
3. **Student-facing register is deferred.** These sentences are working-group-facing. How a Grade-12 student *hears* the question depends on the capstone artifact (E) and phase structure (C), and is not tuned here.
4. **The competency-profiles are entailed, not selection A.** They show what each question would *likely* exercise, as the lens for the gap-test. **A (block-level competency selection)** is finalised *from* **B (the deep question)** *after* the fork — not read off this page.
5. **No verdict is printed.** Every row is descriptive and symmetric. Which trade-offs count as strengths or costs depends on the criterion the group names *after* this silent read (mechanic step 2). The page deliberately does not pre-answer that.

## Side-by-side

| | **Candidate 1 — Epistemological capstone** | **Candidate 2 — Stardust / self ↔ Earth** |
|---|---|---|
| **Illustrative phrasing** *(provisional — not final)* | "How do we know what's out there — what can light tell us, and where does seeing end and inferring begin?" | "We are made of stardust — what does it mean to belong to a cosmos we are materially part of?" |
| **Central object** | Light. Optics treated phenomenologically; cosmology as inference from light. | Cosmos. The student's material relation to the cosmos they are part of. |
| **Centre of gravity** | What scientific knowledge can and cannot establish — the limits of inference, the epistemic stance of the knower. Connecting image: Plato's allegory of the cave (light, shadow, what is and is not directly knowable). | Ecocentric synthesis, responsibility, biographical orientation toward the world; material grounding of cosmic belonging (we are made of stellar matter). |
| **Macro-curriculum fit** | Judgement gr.12 (limits of this model) and the knower-stance application of Future-thinking gr.12. Judgement centre sits at the analytical / epistemic mode — which the macro-curriculum's Judgement row assigns to grade 11 (synthetic/ethical being the grade-12 mode), so the non-binding developmental-pitch question (step 3) may be raised here. Predominance is not exclusivity; a lower judgement mode may legitimately be load-bearing (macro-curriculum's predominance note — mode most centrally at stake, not on/off). | Future-thinking gr.12 (responsibility toward the world) and synthetic judgement. Judgement centre sits at the synthetic mode the macro-curriculum's Judgement row assigns to grade 12. Matches how cosmology was previously taught at the school. |
| **Competency-profile** *(entailed — illustrative, not selection A)* | Leans **S1** (explain phenomena, epistemic-emphasis sub-abilities), **S2** (interpret data / model-limits), **S3** (argument that acknowledges the expert mediation and consensus it depends on — the S3 deepen). | Leans **E1** (human–Earth impact), **E2** (regenerate / sustain), **E3** (respect + hope, socio-ecological), plus **S1 / S2** through the stardust material-science (stellar nucleosynthesis, spectroscopy as evidence of cosmic composition). |
| **What it closes** | Makes the epistemic limits of scientific inference visible; novel framing; keeps optics phenomenologically central. | Engages the environmental strand (E1/E2/E3) directly; ecocentric synthesis and biographical orientation; material-science grounding of "we are made of stardust." |
| **What it leaves open** | The environmental strand (E1/E2/E3) is largely untouched — recorded as a deferred grade-level coverage note, not a veto. The cosmology half turns inferential rather than phenomenological. | The epistemic-limit / inference sub-abilities are largely untouched — recorded as a deferred grade-level coverage note, not a veto. Optics becomes instrumental rather than phenomenologically central. |
| **Classroom-feasibility** | Teacher onboarding: **higher** — a new epistemic framing to build. Optics carries the phenomenological weight; cosmology is taught as inference-from-light. | Teacher onboarding: **lower** — matches prior teaching of cosmology at the school. Optics is taught instrumentally, in service of the cosmological question. |

*"Higher / lower onboarding" is stated as fact, not merit. Whether low onboarding is a virtue or a cost depends on the criterion named in step 2 — e.g. it is a cost under criterion (a) distinctive-new-pedagogy and a virtue under criterion (c) continuity-with-improvement.*
