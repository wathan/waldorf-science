## Motivation and background (context, not action items)
Kru Fang has a research background in Nature of Science (NOS) and graduate-level science education, which is the lens behind this reform. The core diagnosis: the school's existing grade 9–12 science curriculum already has strong Waldorf developmental intentionality — it was never directionless — but it lacks a student-facing central question per grade/thread and a shared competency framework that makes that developmental coherence legible to students themselves. The reform is additive to the existing curriculum, not a correction of an absent framework.

Combined-teaching blocks were originally the intended integration mechanism, but main-lesson scheduling ruled that out — teacher time slots can't accommodate it. The real integrating mechanism became shared question-thread membership and a common competency framework rather than co-taught content, which is why the pilot runs as synchronized, individually-led teaching with rotating "main" roles rather than a single merged block. Anchoring the framework to PISA's scientific literacy competencies (rather than purely Waldorf-internal language) is meant to eventually support university credit recognition, which needs external legibility. A related long-term goal is retrospective recognition: by grade 12, students should be able to look back across four years and consciously recognize, for the first time, what connected their experience the whole time.

### Block design — working group decides; Kru Fang's draft is input only
- Draft deep question: "What does light let us know about the universe — and what does it keep us from knowing?"
- Draft four-phase structure: phenomenology of light (wk 1–2, optics teacher main) → spectroscopy/redshift pivot (wk 3, co-lead) → cosmology by inference (wk 3.5–5.5, cosmology teacher main) → epistemological capstone (final days, co-lead).
- Draft capstone artifact: students sort phenomena from the block into directly-observed / inferred / unknowable — doubles as evidence for the "interpreting data and evidence scientifically" competency strand.
- Teacher-integration mechanism: whichever teacher is secondary in a given phase poses recurring "bridge questions" linking to the other discipline.
- Strong fit found in the macro-curriculum cross-check: optics as the literal self↔whole-world instrument (relational shift), and cosmology recapitulating grade 1's "image of the whole" (spiral pairing). The ethical-judgement gap above is the main open item.
