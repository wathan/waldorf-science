## Part 1 — Proposed Frame (10 min, fast-track)

These six points are the working assumption behind every sub-ability decision in Parts 2–3. Ask if any need to be reopened; otherwise default to ratify and move on.

1. **Architecture.** The six PISA 2025 headline competencies (S1–S3, E1–E3) are preserved verbatim. Grade-12 adaptation lives entirely in the *sub-abilities* under each. (PISA's age-15 calibration is in the sub-abilities, not the headlines.)
2. **Rewrite method.** Per PISA sub-ability, five operations are available — **Keep / Deepen / Shift mode / Add / Drop**. Rule A: default to Keep, justify any change. Rule B: every Add or Shift cites the macro-curriculum row that warrants it.
3. **Purpose ordering.** Primary use = shared spine making blocks legible as one developmental project (including retrospectively to students). Operational use = block-design menu. *Deferred:* graduation rubrics and university-credit mapping.
4. **Vertical anticipation, loose.** Grade-12 mode (synthesis, ethical judgement) is the *predominant* centre of gravity, not an exclusive filter. Earlier modes remain in use where the competency genuinely requires them (macro-curriculum lines 269–271).
5. **Knowledge-type check.** Each headline's Grade-12 sub-ability set must still cover the content / procedural / epistemic mix PISA assigns it, with epistemic emphasised per the grade-11→12 transition.
6. **Block coverage.** Any single block exercises a subset of the framework — full coverage per block is not required. E3 stays socio-ecological per PISA intent; it is not stretched to cover epistemic responsibility.
7. **Operational definitions.** Each sub-ability carries a brief *operationalisation*: what authentic in-classroom evidence would demonstrate it. This sits alongside the sub-ability text in published form. It supports the block-design-menu use (teachers know what they're designing toward) and remains distinct from graduation rubrics (still deferred).

**Macro-citation discipline** (applies to Rule B): the Senses potentiality is about *sensory* perception (gr.12 = recognising the whole through directly-perceived parts). It must not be cited for cognitive representation-handling. Representation work cites *Judgement gr.12* (model-limits) and *grade-11 meta-conceptual competence* carried forward. S3's competency-boundary must also be preserved: S3 keeps the student-as-competent-outsider stance (epistemic dependence on experts and consensus); deepening must not migrate S3 sub-abilities into S2 territory (student-as-investigator).

## Part 2 — Worked Examples

PISA headlines preserved verbatim. Sub-abilities flagged `[Keep]`, `[Deepen]`, `[Shift]`, `[Add]`, `[Drop]`. Macro-curriculum row cited inline for non-Keep.

### S1 — Explain phenomena scientifically

> *Students need to recognise, produce, apply, and evaluate explanations and solutions for a range of natural and technological phenomena and problems.*

1. **[Deepen]** Recall scientific knowledge across previously-separate domains and synthesise it into coherent explanations of complex phenomena.
   *Macro: Holistic thinking, grade 12 — "consciously re-integrating the analysed parts into a coherent, responsible whole."*
   *Operationalisation:* student solves a problem or explains a phenomenon that requires drawing on scientific knowledge from two or more previously-separate units, and names what connects them.

2. **[Deepen]** Translate between different representational modes of a phenomenon, and recognise the strengths and limitations of each representation.
   *Macro: Judgement, grade 12 ("limits of this model") + grade-11 meta-conceptual competence carried forward.*
   *Operationalisation:* student converts between representations (e.g. graph ↔ equation ↔ diagram ↔ words) of the same phenomenon and articulates what each makes visible and what each obscures.

3. **[Deepen]** Make and justify scientific predictions, recognising which model applies to the regime in question and where that model reaches its limits.
   *Macro: Judgement, grade 12 — "limits of this model"; Holistic thinking, grade 12 — drawing on an integrated grasp of multiple models.*
   *Operationalisation:* given a phenomenon, student selects an appropriate model, makes a prediction under it, and identifies the conditions under which the prediction would no longer hold.

4. **[Shift]** Hold multiple models of the same phenomenon in relation, articulating what each captures and what each leaves out, and recognise that holding as one's own constructive achievement.
   *Macro: line 237–238 — "Multiple individuated elements and models … are integrated into a coherent picture, and that integration is recognised as the student's own achievement." Integration here is cognitive holding-in-relation, not operational fusion.*
   *Operationalisation:* student compares two models of the same phenomenon (e.g. wave vs particle picture of light; Newtonian vs relativistic gravity at scale) and states what each captures, what each leaves out, and why both are kept.

5. **[Shift]** Develop explanatory accounts that integrate phenomena from multiple domains into a coherent whole, addressing what stands behind the integrated picture.
   *Macro: Enquiry, grade 12 — "Synthetic: 'Who or what stands behind this whole?'"*
   *Operationalisation:* student writes or presents an explanatory account that brings together phenomena from at least two block-domains under one synthesising frame.

6. **[Shift]** Articulate the social implications of scientific knowledge with *epistemic responsibility as a knower* — clear about what scientific explanation can and cannot establish, and what should not be claimed from it.
   *Macro: Judgement, grade 12 — "limits of this model"; Future thinking, grade 12 — "responsibility toward the world" in its knower-stance application. Actor-responsibility lives in S3, not here.*
   *Operationalisation:* student writes about a science-society question and explicitly states what the relevant science establishes, what it does not, and where over-claiming would begin.

7. **[Add]** Recognise the developmental trajectory of one's own scientific understanding — what one now sees that one earlier could not.
   *Macro: line 222–224 (panoramic retrospective view) + line 249 (spiral pairing: "recapitulates grade 1 … now known consciously, for the first time").*
   *Operationalisation:* student compares their current understanding of a phenomenon to how they understood it earlier (in this block or prior years) and names what changed and why.

**Knowledge-type check:** content + procedural + epistemic all represented; epistemic strongly carried by #2, #3, #4, #6, #7. ✓

### E3 — Demonstrate respect for diverse perspectives, and hope, in seeking solutions to socio-ecological crises

> *Contains elements measured by Science Identity: epistemic beliefs; dispositions of care; feelings of efficacy and agency.*

1. **[Deepen]** Evaluate actions through an ecocentric ethic that the student *can articulate as a developed and owned position* — distinct from unreflective assumption — recognising oneself as located within and responsible toward the whole.
   *Macro: line 232–235 — "consciously held … achieved through conscious synthesis." Contrast: unreflective ecocentric feeling vs articulated ecocentric ethic the student can give reasons for and own.*
   *Operationalisation:* student articulates an ecocentric position in discussion or writing, gives reasons for it, distinguishes it from positions they reject, and acknowledges it as their own developed view rather than an inherited assumption.

2. **[Shift]** Articulate and defend a considered position within the class community on past and present injustices, evaluating actions that empower all people to participate in community and ecosystem well-being.
   *Macro: Democratic participation, grade 12 — "Active: taking up a considered position within a community and contributing to its direction." Phrased for classroom-evidence: position-taking, not real-world action.*
   *Operationalisation:* student presents and defends a position on a real socio-ecological case in seminar or written work; can compare their position to alternatives they considered and rejected; can name what their position commits them to.

3. **[Deepen]** Exhibit resilience, hope, and efficacy as orientations toward the *long-range* world one is about to enter, individually and in concert with others.
   *Macro: Future thinking, grade 12 — "Long-range: 'What is my responsibility toward the world I am about to enter?'"*
   *Operationalisation:* student articulates a long-range orientation in reflective writing or discussion — e.g. on what from this block they expect to carry into adult life and why — without dismissing the difficulty or pretending false certainty.

4. **[Deepen]** Respect diverse perspectives *and integrate them* into a synthetic ethical judgement about what regeneration calls for.
   *Macro: Judgement, grade 12 (synthetic and ethical mode).*
   *Operationalisation:* student weighs multiple stakeholder perspectives on a regeneration case, names what each contributes that the others miss, and arrives at a synthetic ethical position they can defend.

**Knowledge-type check:** dispositional throughout (per PISA); epistemic represented in #1 and #4. ✓

## Part 3 — Pointers (for collaborative rewrite at this meeting)

Per-bullet flag and brief macro-cite for non-Keep. The group rewrites prose together using the method above.

### S2 — Construct/evaluate designs for scientific enquiry; interpret data critically

1. Identify the question in a given scientific study — **[Deepen]** at gr.12 evaluating what the question presupposes / what whole it belongs to. *Enquiry gr.12.*
2. Propose an appropriate experimental design — **[Keep]**, content/procedural foundation.
3. Evaluate whether an experimental design is best suited to the question — **[Shift]** to synthetic judgement across designs incl. limits. *Judgement gr.12.*
4. Interpret data across representations and draw/evaluate conclusions — **[Deepen]** integrating across multiple data representations into a coherent picture, recognising what each representation reveals and obscures. *Holistic thinking gr.12 + grade-11 meta-conceptual competence carried forward.* (Cognitive interpretation, not sensory perception — Senses not cited.)
5. **[Add candidate]** Recognise what a single study can and cannot establish, and integrate findings across studies into a coherent picture. *Holistic thinking gr.12.*

### S3 — Research/evaluate/use scientific information for decisions and action

1. Search/evaluate/communicate relative merits of sources — **[Deepen]** at gr.12 in light of synthetic understanding of how scientific consensus forms. *Macro 192–195.*
2. Distinguish among claims (evidence vs expert vs opinion) — **[Deepen]** as part of meta-conceptual stance toward consensus.
3. Construct an argument from (publicly available) data to a scientific conclusion — **[Deepen]** the argument explicitly acknowledges what expert mediation it depends on and where the consensus it draws on comes from. *Judgement gr.12.* (Stays in S3's competent-outsider stance — argument-from-own-investigation belongs to S2.)
4. Critique standard flaws in science-related arguments — **[Keep]**, procedural+epistemic capacity persists.
5. Justify decisions using scientific arguments contributing to sustainable development — **[Shift]** to ethical-synthetic. *Judgement gr.12 + Democratic participation gr.12.*

### E1 — Explain the impact of human interactions with Earth's systems

1. Explain physical/living/Earth systems and their interactions — **[Deepen]** at gr.12 held synthetically as one whole. *Holistic thinking gr.12.*
2. Research/apply knowledge of human interactions over time — **[Deepen]** long-range time-thinking. *Future thinking gr.12.*
3. Apply knowledge to explain positive/negative human impacts — **[Keep]**, content-foundational.
4. Explain how social/cultural/economic factors contribute — **[Deepen]** synthesis across natural and cultural factors. *Holistic thinking gr.12.*

### E2 — Make informed decisions to regenerate and sustain the environment

1. Search/evaluate evidence from diverse knowledge systems — **[Deepen]** synthetic evaluation across knowledge systems.
2. Evaluate/design solutions using creative and systems thinking for current and future generations — **[Keep]**, language already grade-12-flavoured.
3. Engage in civic processes for informed, consensual decisions — **[Shift]** to active position-taking. *Democratic participation gr.12.*
4. Set goals, collaborate across generations, act for regenerative socio-ecological change — **[Keep]**, language already grade-12-flavoured.
