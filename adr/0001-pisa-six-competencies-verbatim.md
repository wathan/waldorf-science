# Adopt PISA 2025's six competencies verbatim as Grade 12 framework headlines

## Context

The Grade 12 science reform needs a shared competency framework that (a) makes
four years of disparate blocks legible as one developmental project, including
retrospectively to students, and (b) eventually supports external recognition
including university credit. PISA 2025 defines six competencies — three science
(S1 Explain phenomena, S2 Construct/evaluate inquiry and interpret data,
S3 Research/evaluate/use information for decisions) and three environmental
science (E1 Explain human–Earth-system interactions, E2 Make informed
decisions, E3 Demonstrate respect/hope) — calibrated to age 15 but framed as
an age-independent model of scientific literacy as a citizen quality.

PISA's age-15 calibration is in its *sub-abilities*, not its headline
competencies. The headlines are the age-independent layer.

## Decision

The Grade 12 framework preserves all six PISA 2025 headline competencies
verbatim (S1, S2, S3, E1, E2, E3). The Grade-12-specific adaptation lives
entirely in the rewriting of *sub-abilities* under each headline, per the
five-operation method (Keep, Deepen, Shift mode, Add, Drop) recorded in
[ADR-0002 if drafted later].

## Considered alternatives

- **Extend PISA in place** (chosen): headlines verbatim, sub-abilities
  rewritten for Grade-12 mode.
- **PISA as floor, Grade 12 as superset**: keep PISA's six and add a seventh
  Grade-12-only competency for synthetic/ethical judgement. Rejected because
  the gap it addresses can be discharged inside the existing six (E3 carries
  socio-ecological ethics; Grade-12-mode sub-abilities under S1–S3 can carry
  epistemic responsibility; macro-curriculum line 65 already names the
  synthetic-and-ethical judgement that closes the gap).
- **Re-derive from PISA's stated intent**: use the citizen-scientific-literacy
  goal but reconstruct headline competencies from Waldorf grade-12 themes.
  Rejected because it sacrifices the external-legibility property that
  motivated PISA anchoring in the first place.

## Consequences

- **External legibility preserved.** Any reader who knows PISA can map the
  framework onto PISA's six. This is the property the future credit-recognition
  goal depends on, even though that goal is currently deferred.
- **Constraint on rewriting.** Grade-12 sub-abilities under each headline must
  remain coherent with PISA's stated competency intent. A sub-ability that
  drifts away from "explain phenomena scientifically" (S1's headline) cannot be
  placed under S1 even if it is otherwise Grade-12-appropriate; it must live
  under a different headline or be dropped.
- **No new headline for the ethical-judgement gap.** The gap flagged in the
  Jun-16 handoff (macro-curriculum's synthetic-*and-ethical* Grade-12
  judgement) is closed through sub-ability rewrites within the six, not by
  adding a seventh competency. E3 is not stretched beyond its socio-ecological
  intent to cover this.
- **Blocks exercise subsets.** A single block need not exercise all six
  competencies. The framework is the shared spine; blocks select from it.
