# Grade-12 action/ethics sub-abilities written as in-community position-taking; assessment locus deferred to operationalisation

## Context

Several PISA sub-abilities under S3, E2 and E3 are action-oriented: justify
decisions that contribute to contemporary issues (S3.5), engage in civic
processes (E2.3), act for socio-ecological change "local to global" (E2.4),
work to empower all people (E3.2). Rewriting these for the Grade-12 framework
forced two coupled questions: (a) how far toward *real-world civic action*
should the ability *statement* reach, and (b) where does the
classroom/seminar assessment bound live — in the statement or elsewhere?

The macro-curriculum row these SHIFTs cite, Democratic participation gr.12
(`source/waldorf-upper-school-macro-curriculum.md:68`), reads: *"Active:
taking up a considered position within a community and contributing to its
direction."* — position-taking within a community, not real-world action.

## Decision

The ability **statement** is written to that macro mode: real-world subject
matter (civic decisions, injustices, sustainability) but the student's act is
*taking up and defending a considered position within a community*. It does
**not** assert real-world civic action as an expectation. The classroom/seminar
assessment locus is **not** stated in the description — it lives only in the
*operationalisation* (Evidence-shape), which is currently deferred per schedule.

General principle: **the ability description carries the macro developmental
mode; the operationalisation carries the assessment locus.** The two are not
mixed.

E2.4 retains PISA's real-world "local to global" wording solely because it is a
`KEEP` (verbatim source text), not a fresh Grade-12 claim that would need a
macro warrant.

## Considered alternatives

- **Real-world reach in the description + a note deferring the classroom bound**
  (rejected): over-claims past the macro warrant (violates the framework's own
  Rule B — every SHIFT cites the macro row that warrants it); contradicts the
  already-drafted E3.2 exemplar; and the note is itself deferred
  operationalisation, a promise with no enforcing teeth that invites real-world
  assessment drift in review.
- **Generic real-world-action statements** (rejected): not assessable inside a
  block, and the framework is a shared spine plus block-design menu, not a
  civic-action mandate.

## Consequences

- **Retro-edit to E3.2.** Its statement smuggled the locus in
  ("within the *class* community"); changed to "within a community". The
  classroom locus remains in E3.2's Evidence-shape ("in seminar or written
  work"). E3.1/E3.3/E3.4 statements carry no locus and are untouched.
- **Consistent exemplar shape.** Reviewers pattern-matching the S1/E3 worked
  examples get the same description-vs-operationalisation division everywhere.
- **Binds the deferred operationalisation phase.** When operationalisation is
  drafted for S2/S3/E1/E2, the classroom locus is its job — not a reopening of
  the statements.
